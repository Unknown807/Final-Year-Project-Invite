Description

The final year project as written on the Kent website:

"Students, working in small groups, undertake a project related to computer science and/or software engineering. The project may be self-proposed or may be selected from a list of project proposals. A project will involve the specification, design, implementation, documentation and demonstration of a technical artefact, demonstrating the ability to synthesise information, ideas and practices to provide a quality solution together with an evaluation of that solution."

The aim of this invite is to reach out to people who are doing a year in industry or another year in education, and get them to join our current team for the final year project (2023-2024). It may seem quite early for assembling teams, but given the deadlines of registering projects at the latest in September this year (2022) and the limited spaces available for supervision by lecturers (especially desirable ones), we feel it best to start now and get ahead.

Requirements

Mandatory

  • Proficient in Object-Oriented Programming
  • Firm grasp of software architecture fundamentals
  • Solid understanding of best programming practices (documentation, decoupling, debugging, testing, etc.)
  • Adept at using version control (git, branching, good commit frequency, etc.)
  • Good work ethic
  • Quick at learning and adapting
  • Effective team coordination skills
  • Advanced English Level

Desirable

  • A grade of 2:1 and above from first year
  • Advanced Java knowledge
  • Highly skilled in relational database design and implementation (MySQL, PostgreSQL, SQL Server)
  • Proficient in using the basic web technologies (HTML, CSS, JS)
  • Another skill/specialisation (e.g. graphic design)
  • Online showcase of personal projects, code, art, or similar
